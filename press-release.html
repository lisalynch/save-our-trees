Residents launch campaign against Irish Rail access road that would destroy private gardens and fell mature native trees

Ballyrichard community calls on Irish Rail to choose one of several alternative routes before the Railway Order is filed

CARRIGTWOHILL, Co. Cork — Residents of the Ballyrichard area have launched a public campaign opposing Irish Rail's preferred route for a workers' access road to its new CACR Phase 2 depot, warning that the chosen option would permanently destroy private residential gardens and fell mature native oak and ash trees that have stood for generations.

Irish Rail assessed eight options for the access road as part of its Cork Area Commuter Rail Programme Public Consultation No. 2. The project team selected Option 7 — a route that passes directly through the private gardens of local families — over several alternatives that would reach the depot without affecting a single home.

The residents' own analysis of Irish Rail's published report reveals that Option 7 was chosen over the next-best alternative on what the project team itself described as a "narrow" margin.

"We fully support the Cork Area Commuter Rail Programme. What we cannot accept is the permanent loss of our gardens, our trees, and our privacy to provide a staff access road, when Irish Rail's own engineers identified alternatives that avoid all of that. We are asking them to look again."

— Lisa Lynch, local resident, Ballyrichard

If Option 7 proceeds, residents say it will also bring round-the-clock traffic to Waterrock Road as depot workers travel to and from 160 car parking spaces across multiple shift changes, and it will route drainage infrastructure along the access road to Castle Rock Avenue — raising concerns about flood risk in Midleton.

The campaign has launched at no2option7.org, where local people can send an objection to Irish Rail in under two minutes. The written consultation deadline is Friday 12 June 2026.
